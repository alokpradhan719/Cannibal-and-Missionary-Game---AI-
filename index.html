Window 01 — Introduction
The Cannibals & Missionary Problem
A foundational puzzle in Artificial Intelligence exploring state-space search and constraint satisfaction.
The Problem

A group of N missionaries and N cannibals must cross a river using a single boat that holds at most 2 people. The boat cannot cross the river empty — someone must always row.

The critical constraint: cannibals must never outnumber missionaries on either bank. If they do, the missionaries are in danger and the crossing fails.

This puzzle is a classic AI search problem, solved optimally using Breadth-First Search (BFS) — the same algorithm used in this game's Auto-Solve feature.

Window 02 — Constraints & Rules
Problem Constraints & Rules of Play
These constraints define every valid game state. Violating any one of them ends the crossing in failure.
Formal Constraints
C1
Boat Capacity Constraint
Each crossing must carry 1 or 2 people. Formally: 1 ≤ passengers ≤ 2. The boat cannot cross empty and cannot carry more than 2.
C2
Safety Constraint — Bank A (Left)
If missionaries are present on Bank A, cannibals must not outnumber them. Formally: ML = 0 OR CL ≤ ML.
C3
Safety Constraint — Bank B (Right)
Same rule applies to the right bank simultaneously. Formally: MR = 0 OR CR ≤ MR. Both banks checked after every crossing.
C4
Conservation Constraint
Total counts are always preserved: ML + MR = N and CL + CR = N. No one is created or lost.
C5
Non-Negativity Constraint
All counts must be non-negative: ML, CL, MR, CR ≥ 0. You cannot send more people than are present on a bank.
